ROSHEN Confectionery Corporation is one of the largest world manufacturers of confectionery products.
According to the world rating «Candy Industry TOP-100», Roshen ranks among the top-20.

ROSHEN produces over 320 kinds of confectionery products: chocolate bars, chocolate and jelly sweets, hard candies, biscuits, sponge cakes, wafers. Total overall production is over 450 thousand tons per year.

To remain a leader, just moving forward is not enough; one must set the pace for the whole industry. That is why ROSHEN’s operation has always been firmly based on two fundamentals – quality and innovation.

All ROSHEN factories are provided with state-of-the-art equipment of the world’s leading manufacturers.
ROSHEN Corporation has 7 production factories – in Lithuania, Hungary, Russia and Ukraine – and exports its products to 30 countries around the world.

Every year, ROSHEN develops and launches new products.

The number of employees in ROSHEN, including its offices and production facilities in Ukraine, Hungary, Lithuania, Russia, Kazakhstan, China and other countries, exceeds 10 000.

All ROSHEN factories equipped with high production modern machinery from the leading world suppliers. New lines and production of new types of products is a must.

Quality management systems ISO 9001:2008, Food safety management systems ISO 22000:2005 and IFS international standards are implemented at all ROSHEN factories.

- ROSHEN Kyiv Confectionery Factory (Ukraine) is specialized in the production of chocolate sweets, fresh cakes and pastries, biscuits, crackers, marmalade, soufflé products and it produces circa 150 kinds of confectionery products. The factory starts its history from the 19th century, it was founded in 1886.

- ROSHEN Vinnitsa Confectionery Factory (Ukraine) produces wafers and jelly products, chocolate-waffle cakes, biscuits, Swiss rolls and cakes, chocolate sweets, bars, and tablets, including aerated ones. The factory has been producing confectionery starting from 1929. In 2012 ROSHEN Corporation launched second production site in Vinnitsa, Ukraine, which increases the overall production by 120 thousand tons of confectionery products per year.

- ROSHEN Kremenchug Confectionery Factory (Ukraine) is specialized in the production of caramel products. The plant produces more than 30 items – hard, aerated candies, hard candies with various fillings – milk, fruit, fizzy. The factory is one of the oldest in Ukraine, the first candies were produced in 1898.

- ROSHEN Lipetsk Confectionery Factory (Russia) manufactures its products at two plants (in Lipetsk and v. Sentsovo): several types of caramel, jelly sweets, toffee, biscuits, glazed sweets with nougat, wafer cakes.

- ROSHEN Klaipeda Confectionery Factory (Lithuania) is specialized in the manufacturing of caramel products. Its history is longer than 100 years. In 2011 modernization of the factory was completed, it was equipped with exclusively new machinery - new lines were installed for production of hard candies with and without fillers, milk caramel.

- Company’s Hungarian production facilities Bonbonetti Choco Kft. are located in Budapest. The factory in Budapest was founded back in 1868 and for the last 150 years it’s been the largest producer of chocolate confectioneries in Hungary. As long ago as 1934, Bonbonetti products were popular not only domestically, but also on the markets of other European countries. In 2014 ROSHEN Corporation launches a milk processing plant which will provide Corporation with high quality milk products.

Twenty-four/seven, every six minutes, one 53-foot trailer loaded with confectionery goods leaves ROSHEN’s warehouse. The daily mileage of 82 ROSHEN trailer trucks adds up to a distance surpassing the length of the Equator. The monthly mileage of the ROSHEN fleet exceeds 1 million and 500 000 kilometers. Each vehicle is equipped with an advanced commodity flow control-and-management system. Therefore, we can control the quality of goods not only during production, but also throughout delivery.
Within one working shift, ROSHEN produces over one million kilos of confectionery. To keep up the pace, the company developed and constructed most advanced logistic centres in the towns of Yahotin (Ukraine) and Lipetsk (Russia).
The centres meet all the standards of A Class logistics complex and are equipped with the advanced equipment.
The storage area of the Lipetsk distribution Centre is 32 000 square meters, with a 62 000-pallet capacity.
Total area of the Yahotin center is 60,000 m2. Pallet capacity is 41 thousand pallets of ready-made products and 26 thousand pallets of raw materials.
